Key Information on Agriculture

Agricultural development is a key policy priority for the Government to pave the way for the Island to become more self sufficient in the production of vegetables, meat, fruit and fish. The Agriculture and Natural Resources Directorate (ANRD), together with the St Helena Development Agency (SHDA) strives to encourage and support private sector development in these areas through the implementation of various policies and direct funding support. In the meantime, the majority of our fruit and more than half of our vegetables are imported to the Island. Imports of fresh fruit and vegetable produce are allowed into the Island providing strict phytosanitary health conditions are met by importers.

To import animals, plant material, animals and bee products and equipment into the Island see the linked Plant Material Imports Policy, the Plant Material for Growing Purposes Health Standard, Import Health Standard for Cats and Dogs from UK (United Kingdom) and ROI (Republic of Ireland) and Bee Products and Equipment Health Standard.

ANRD also provides a number of supporting services to producers which includes: irrigation services, pesticides spraying services, farmer extension support services, livestock and poultry veterinary and nucleus bloodline service, animal and plant biosecurity services and leasing of agricultural land and buildings for production.
